# PART I — FOUNDATIONS

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This Operations Manual codifies **how FINBERG ADVISORY OÜ runs**. It converts the strategic intent of Manual 01 into the day-to-day procedures, workflows, controls, and standards by which engagements are won, delivered, and closed.

**Scope.** All operational activities of the Firm — from the moment a lead is captured to the moment a closed engagement is archived and a client relationship enters renewal motion. The manual covers:

- Origination, qualification, proposal, and contracting
- Engagement delivery from kickoff to closure
- Internal approvals, document management, quality control
- Client communication, escalation, and complaints
- Daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, and annual operating cadence
- Internal reporting and KPIs
- Automation roadmap

**Out of scope.** Sales tactics and outreach motion (Manual 03), marketing and content (Manual 04), financial controls (Manual 05), HR processes (Manual 06), legal/compliance frameworks (Manual 07), AI delivery stack (Manual 08), technology infrastructure (Manual 09), and the firm-wide Knowledge Base (Manual 10).

**Audience.** Every person on every engagement, regardless of seniority, contractor status, or location.

## 2. Operating Principles

Ten non-negotiable operating principles. They sit above any specific procedure: if a procedure ever conflicts with a principle, the principle wins and the procedure must be amended.

1. **Diagnose before prescribing.** No recommendation, no slide, no decision until the structured diagnosis is documented and signed-off.
2. **Partner attends.** A partner attends every client meeting. There is no "associate-only" client touchpoint.
3. **One slide, one message.** Every slide has a single, defensible title-claim supported by the page.
4. **Data, then opinion.** Every claim cites a source, a calculation, or a labeled assumption.
5. **Three options, not one.** Strategic recommendations are framed as options with trade-offs, never as a single take-it-or-leave-it answer.
6. **Document everything client-facing.** Meeting → minutes; deliverable → version log; commitment → owner + date.
7. **Two-pizza teams.** Engagement teams 3–6 people. Quality scales through senior leverage, not analyst headcount.
8. **The 24-hour rule.** Every client message acknowledged within 24 hours, regardless of substantive answer.
9. **Friday review.** Every active engagement gets a 30-minute internal quality review every Friday.
10. **Kill your darlings.** Anything not serving the client outcome is removed, no matter how hard we worked on it.

## 3. How to Use this Manual

The manual is structured for two modes of use.

- **Reference mode.** Look up a specific process, template, or KPI. Use the Table of Contents and the appendices.
- **Read-through mode.** New joiners read Parts I and II in full during onboarding (covered in Manual 06), and Parts III–V before their first proposal cycle.

Each part follows the same pattern:

- **Purpose** — what this part exists to enable.
- **Process** — the procedural detail.
- **RACI** — who does what.
- **Standards and KPIs** — how we measure quality.
- **Templates** — what to use.
- **Anti-patterns** — what to avoid.

# PART II — ENGAGEMENT LIFECYCLE (THE CORE FLOW)

## 6. Engagement Lifecycle Overview

Every engagement passes through ten stages. Each stage has entry criteria, exit criteria, deliverables, owners, KPIs, and templates.

### 6.1 The Ten Stages

| Stage | Name | Purpose | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | Origination & Qualification | Convert raw lead to qualified opportunity | 1–4 weeks |
| 1 | Discovery & Scoping | Understand the real problem | 1–3 weeks |
| 2 | Proposal & Contracting | Agree what we do, for how much, with what risks | 1–4 weeks |
| 3 | Kickoff | Align team and client; commit cadence | 1 week |
| 4 | Diagnosis | Establish what is actually true | 2–6 weeks |
| 5 | Recommendation | Present defensible options and decision | 1–3 weeks |
| 6 | Implementation Support | Help client execute | 4–24 weeks (variable) |
| 7 | Closure & Handover | Hand over assets and learnings | 1–2 weeks |
| 8 | Post-Engagement Care | Sustain the relationship | Indefinite |
| 9 | Reference, Cross-Sell, Renewal | Compound the relationship | Indefinite |

### 6.2 Stage-Gate Discipline

Between every stage there is a **stage gate**: a documented checkpoint where the next stage cannot start until the previous stage's exit criteria are met and a designated approver signs off.

Gate failures fall back to the same stage (do it again better) — they never skip backward more than one stage, and they never skip forward.

### 6.3 Lifecycle Flowchart (textual)

```
LEAD → [Gate 0a: Qualified?] → DISCOVERY → [Gate 1: Scoping signed?] → PROPOSAL → 
[Gate 2: EL signed?] → KICKOFF → [Gate 3: Plan agreed?] → DIAGNOSIS → 
[Gate 4: Diagnosis signed by client?] → RECOMMENDATION → 
[Gate 5: Client decision recorded?] → IMPLEMENTATION SUPPORT → 
[Gate 6: Milestones delivered?] → CLOSURE → [Gate 7: Handover accepted?] → 
POST-ENGAGEMENT CARE → REFERENCE / CROSS-SELL / RENEWAL
```

## 7. Stage 0 — Origination and Qualification

### 7.1 Purpose

Turn a raw lead into a **qualified opportunity** worth investing scoping time in.

### 7.2 Inputs

- Inbound inquiry, referral, RFP, or partner-originated lead
- Lead source captured in CRM
- Basic firm and industry context

### 7.3 Process

1. **Lead capture (T+0)** — Inbound goes to the Inbox Owner (rotating); CRM record created within 24 hours.
2. **Triage (T+1 day)** — Inbox Owner classifies: Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3 / Out-of-scope.
3. **Assignment (T+1 day)** — Tier 1 and Tier 2 routed to relevant Practice Leader or Sector Leader.
4. **First contact (T+3 days)** — Assigned partner makes contact (call or written) within 72 hours.
5. **Qualification call (T+5–10 days)** — 30–60 minute call with the buyer-side contact.
6. **Qualification scoring** — using the BANT-CHAMP-MEDDIC composite below.
7. **Decision (T+10 days)** — Qualified → Stage 1. Not qualified → polite decline, logged.

### 7.4 Qualification Framework (FINBERG Q-SCORE)

A weighted composite of seven dimensions, each scored 1–5:

| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategic fit** | 20% | Aligns with target segments (Manual 01 §18) |
| **Problem clarity** | 15% | Buyer can articulate the problem (or is willing to let us diagnose) |
| **Budget reality** | 15% | A realistic budget exists or can be built |
| **Decision authority** | 15% | The contact owns the decision or is one step away |
| **Timing** | 10% | A real decision window exists in the next 6 months |
| **Capacity to engage** | 10% | Client has internal capacity to absorb advisory work |
| **Reputational fit** | 15% | Client and engagement enhance the brand |

Scores 28+ (out of 35) → Tier 1. 21–27 → Tier 2. < 21 → decline or nurture.

### 7.5 Exit Criteria (Gate 0a → Stage 1)

- Q-Score ≥ 21
- Conflict check passed (§19)
- Engagement Partner assigned
- CRM updated to "Qualified Opportunity"

### 7.6 RACI

| Activity | R | A | C | I |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lead capture | Inbox Owner | COO | - | CRM |
| Triage | Inbox Owner | COO | Practice Leader | - |
| Qualification call | EP candidate | EP candidate | - | CRM |
| Q-Score | EP candidate | Practice Leader | - | OpsCo |

### 7.7 KPIs

- Lead-to-first-contact time (target: < 72h, world-class < 24h)
- Lead-to-qualification time (target: < 14d)
- Qualification rate (% leads passing Q-Score; healthy: 25–40%)
- Conversion to discovery (target: > 60% of qualified opportunities)

### 7.8 Templates / Tools

- **CRM Lead Form** (Manual 09 specifies the system; field list in Appendix)
- **Qualification Call Guide** (Appendix J — SOP-OPS-001)
- **Decline Email Template** (Appendix B variants)

### 7.9 Anti-patterns

- Pursuing leads where the Q-Score is below threshold to "fill the funnel"
- Allowing a junior to make first contact
- Skipping the conflict check
- Promising specifics in qualification before scoping

## 8. Stage 1 — Discovery and Scoping

### 8.1 Purpose

Understand the **real problem** behind the surface problem, and shape a scope of work that is achievable, valuable, and defensible.

### 8.2 Inputs

- Qualified Opportunity from Stage 0
- Client briefing documents
- Buyer-side initial materials (deck, data room, financials, prior reports)

### 8.3 Process

1. **Scoping kickoff (internal)** — EP, EM-candidate, and Practice Leader define scoping hypotheses.
2. **Buyer-side interviews** — Typically 3–8 interviews with sponsor, key stakeholders, and operational owners.
3. **Existing-material review** — Read existing strategies, prior reports, board minutes (where shared), financials.
4. **Hypothesis tree** — Structured tree of possible root causes / problems.
5. **Scope shaping** — Convert hypotheses into a clear engagement scope, with explicit out-of-scope items.
6. **Internal scope review** — Practice Leader sign-off.
7. **Scope confirmation with client** — Written scope memo (1–2 pages) signed by client sponsor.

### 8.4 The Discovery Hypothesis Tree (template)

```
PRESENTING PROBLEM
├── HYPOTHESIS A: …
│   ├── Evidence to gather: …
│   └── Implication if true: …
├── HYPOTHESIS B: …
│   ├── Evidence to gather: …
│   └── Implication if true: …
└── HYPOTHESIS C: …
```

The tree must be MECE (mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive) at each level.

### 8.5 Exit Criteria (Gate 1 → Stage 2)

- Written scope memo signed by client sponsor
- Hypothesis tree documented internally
- Indicative fee range agreed verbally
- Conflicts re-checked
- Internal EP + Practice Leader sign-off

### 8.6 RACI

| Activity | R | A | C | I |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Scoping kickoff | EM | EP | Practice Leader | OpsCo |
| Interviews | EP + EM | EP | - | CRM |
| Hypothesis tree | EM | EP | Practice Leader | - |
| Scope memo | EM | EP | Practice Leader, CLO if material risk | CRM |

### 8.7 KPIs

- Discovery duration (target: ≤ 3 weeks)
- Conversion to proposal (target: > 75%)
- Client sign-off on scope memo before proposal: 100%

### 8.8 Templates / Tools

- **Scoping Interview Guide** (SOP-OPS-002)
- **Hypothesis Tree Template**
- **Scope Memo Template** (1–2 pages)

### 8.9 Anti-patterns

- Skipping interviews to "save time"
- Letting the client pre-define the scope without our diagnosis
- Promising deliverables before scope sign-off
- Hypotheses that are not MECE

## 9. Stage 2 — Proposal and Contracting

### 9.1 Purpose

Convert the agreed scope into a **signed engagement** with clear terms, pricing, deliverables, governance, and risk allocation.

### 9.2 Process

1. **Proposal kickoff (internal)** — EP, EM, Pricing reviewer.
2. **Drafting (T+3–5 days)** — EM drafts; EP reviews structure and pricing.
3. **Internal review** — Practice Leader, CFO (if pricing outside standard), CLO (if non-standard risk).
4. **Proposal delivery (T+5–10 days)** — EP presents in person or video; deck + cover letter + EL terms.
5. **Negotiation (T+1–4 weeks)** — EP leads commercial conversation; CFO and CLO consulted on substantive changes.
6. **EL signature** — Counter-signed by EP and counter-signed by client signatory.
7. **Internal mobilization** — Engagement code created; staffing confirmed; CRM updated to "Won".

### 9.3 Proposal Structure (Standard)

1. **Cover letter (1 page)** — addressed to the buyer; signed by EP.
2. **Executive Summary (1–2 pages)**
3. **Our Understanding of Your Problem** (2–3 pages)
4. **Approach and Methodology** (3–5 pages)
5. **Workplan and Timeline** (Gantt-style, 1 page)
6. **Team and Roles** (1–2 pages, with bios)
7. **Deliverables** (explicit list)
8. **Commercials and Pricing** (1 page)
9. **Governance and Communication** (1 page)
10. **Risks, Assumptions, Out-of-Scope** (1 page)
11. **Why FINBERG** (1 page — positioning recap)
12. **Engagement Letter Terms** (annex)

### 9.4 Exit Criteria (Gate 2 → Stage 3)

- Signed Engagement Letter (or counter-signed MSA + Statement of Work)
- Engagement code created (Manual 05)
- Staffing confirmed and calendared
- Internal kickoff scheduled within 5 working days

### 9.5 KPIs

- Proposal-to-decision time (target: ≤ 21 days)
- Win rate (target: > 50% on qualified opportunities)
- Price realization vs. standard band (target: ≥ 100%)
- Zero engagements started without signed EL

### 9.6 Anti-patterns

- "Light" proposals without commercials defined
- Verbal-only changes after sign-off
- Starting work before signature ("we'll catch up the paperwork later")
- Discount escalation without strategic rationale

## 10. Stage 3 — Kickoff

### 10.1 Purpose

Translate the signed engagement into an **operating mode**: aligned team, aligned client, agreed cadence.

### 10.2 Process

1. **Internal kickoff (Day 0)** — EP + EM + team; review scope, workplan, risks, RACI.
2. **Engagement folder setup (Day 0)** — Standard structure (§51); workplan loaded; RAID log opened.
3. **Client kickoff meeting (Day 1–3)** — 90–120 minutes; sponsor + key client stakeholders + engagement team.
4. **Cadence agreement** — Weekly status, biweekly steering, Friday review (internal).
5. **Interview / data request list** — Issued to client within 48 hours.
6. **Communication plan** — Single point of contact on each side, response time commitments, channels.

### 10.3 Client Kickoff Meeting Agenda (template)

1. Introductions and roles (15 min)
2. Scope, objectives, deliverables (15 min)
3. Workplan and milestones (20 min)
4. Governance, cadence, communication (10 min)
5. Data, interviews, access requests (15 min)
6. Risks, assumptions, dependencies (10 min)

### 10.4 Exit Criteria (Gate 3 → Stage 4)

- Client kickoff completed; minutes circulated within 24h and signed back
- Engagement folder live with workplan, RAID log, communication plan
- Interview/data request issued and acknowledged
- Engagement Steering scheduled

### 10.5 RACI

| Activity | R | A | C | I |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Internal kickoff | EM | EP | Team | OpsCo |
| Folder setup | EM | EM | Knowledge Lead | - |
| Client kickoff | EP + EM | EP | Practice Leader | CRM |
| Cadence | EM | EP | Client sponsor | Team |

### 10.6 KPIs

- Kickoff completed within 5 wd of EL signature (100%)
- Minutes circulated within 24h (100%)
- All Day-1 commitments tracked in RAID (100%)

## 11. Stage 4 — Diagnosis

### 11.1 Purpose

Establish what is **actually true** about the client situation, in a way the client trusts.

### 11.2 Process

1. **Hypothesis-driven analysis** — Refine the hypothesis tree from Stage 1 into testable propositions.
2. **Evidence collection** — Interviews, data analysis, document review, benchmarking, expert calls.
3. **Synthesis** — Convert evidence into structured findings.
4. **Triangulation** — At least two independent sources per material finding.
5. **Diagnosis deck (interim)** — Mid-stage check-in with client sponsor; surface emerging picture.
6. **Diagnosis deck (final)** — Comprehensive diagnosis presented and signed off by client.

### 11.3 Diagnosis Standards

- **Every finding has a source.** Citation in footer.
- **Every chart has a labeled axis and source.**
- **Every claim has a labeled assumption or evidence anchor.**
- **No "consultant-speak"** — plain language only.
- **MECE structure** for all summaries.

### 11.4 Mid-Stage Check-in

A non-negotiable interim review with the client sponsor, typically at 40–60% of diagnosis effort. Purpose:

- Validate emerging direction.
- Surface disagreements early.
- Avoid surprises at final.

### 11.5 Exit Criteria (Gate 4 → Stage 5)

- Final diagnosis deck delivered
- Client sponsor written acknowledgment (email or signed sign-off)
- All sources and data citations archived in engagement folder
- Internal Friday review confirms no open quality issues

### 11.6 RACI

| Activity | R | A | C | I |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hypothesis refinement | EM | EP | Practice Leader | - |
| Analysis | EM + Consultants | EP | - | - |
| Triangulation | EM | EP | - | - |
| Mid-stage check-in | EP + EM | EP | Client sponsor | Steering |
| Final diagnosis | EP + EM | EP | Practice Leader | Client sponsor |

### 11.7 KPIs

- On-time mid-stage check-in (100%)
- Triangulation rate (% material findings with ≥ 2 sources) — target: 100%
- Client sign-off on diagnosis: 100% before recommendation

### 11.8 Anti-patterns

- "Boiling-the-ocean" research without hypothesis discipline
- Skipping mid-stage check-in to "surprise" the client
- Single-source findings presented as fact
- Burying disagreements until final

## 12. Stage 5 — Recommendation

### 12.1 Purpose

Convert diagnosis into **three structured options with trade-offs** and support the client through a decision.

### 12.2 Process

1. **Options generation** — At least three distinct options. Each option specifies what changes, what stays, what it costs, what it delivers.
2. **Trade-off analysis** — Side-by-side comparison: time, cost, risk, capability requirement, expected impact.
3. **Recommendation (with reasoning)** — We may state our preferred option, but we always present the others fairly.
4. **Decision workshop with client** — Structured workshop to arrive at decision.
5. **Decision memo** — Documented outcome with decision rationale.

### 12.3 The Options Comparison Template

|---|---|---|---|
| What changes | … | … | … |
| What stays | … | … | … |
| Time | … | … | … |
| Cost | … | … | … |

| Expected impact (range) | … | … | … |
| Key risks | … | … | … |
| Reversibility | … | … | … |
| Pre-commitments needed | … | … | … |

### 12.4 Exit Criteria (Gate 5 → Stage 6)

- Decision memo signed by client
- Decision recorded in engagement folder
- Implementation scope agreed (or implementation handed back to client if not in scope)
- NPS pulse taken if engagement ends here

### 12.5 RACI

| Activity | R | A | C | I |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Options generation | EP + EM | EP | Practice Leader | - |
| Trade-off analysis | EM | EP | - | - |
| Decision workshop | EP | EP | Client sponsor | Steering |
| Decision memo | EM | EP | CLO if material | All |

### 12.6 KPIs

- Three or more options always presented (100%)
- Decision memo within 5 wd of workshop (100%)
- Client sign-off on decision (100%)

## 13. Stage 6 — Implementation Support

### 13.1 Purpose

Help the client execute the decision with discipline, momentum, and measurable progress.

### 13.2 Scope (varies by engagement)

Implementation support may include any of:
- Program design and PMO
- Change management
- Capability building (training, coaching, hiring support)
- Process redesign and rollout
- KPI design and dashboard setup
- Vendor selection support
- Interim leadership support

### 13.3 Process

1. **Implementation charter** — Translates decision into a structured execution plan.
2. **Governance setup** — Steering, working groups, RACI, cadence.
3. **Workstream execution** — Time-boxed sprints with defined outcomes.
4. **Weekly status** — Workstream-level status to Steering.
5. **Milestone reviews** — Every 4–6 weeks: re-confirm direction, surface issues, re-plan.
6. **Quality gates** — Independent review at key milestones.

### 13.4 Implementation Discipline Rules

- **No "consultant-only" workstreams.** Every workstream has a named client lead.
- **No "shadow implementation."** We do not silently do the client's job; we make capability visible.
- **No scope drift.** Changes require Change Control (§41).
- **Decisions logged.** Implementation decisions are logged in a Decision Register.

### 13.5 Exit Criteria (Gate 6 → Stage 7)

- All committed milestones delivered or formally re-scoped via Change Control
- Implementation outcomes summarized
- Client lead trained and capable to continue
- NPS pulse taken

### 13.6 KPIs

- Milestone on-time rate (target: ≥ 85%)
- Change Control discipline (100% of scope changes via formal process)
- Client lead self-sufficiency (assessed at close)

## 14. Stage 7 — Closure and Handover

### 14.1 Purpose

Close the engagement cleanly — financially, legally, operationally, and reputationally — and hand over enduring assets to the client.

### 14.2 Process

1. **Close meeting with client** — Recap of objectives, outcomes, what worked, what would be done differently next time.
2. **Final deliverable package** — All artifacts in a single client-handover bundle.
3. **Asset handover** — Models, dashboards, frameworks delivered with documentation.
4. **Knowledge transfer sessions** — Training and walkthroughs for client team.
5. **Financial close** — Final invoice, retention amount released (if any), reconciliation.
6. **Legal close** — Confidentiality continuation; document retention compliance.
7. **Internal post-mortem** — Within 10 working days of close.
8. **NPS survey** — Sent within 2 working days of close.

### 14.3 Final Deliverable Bundle Standard

- All client-facing decks (final versions, dated)
- Executive summary memo (1–3 pages) summarizing engagement and outcomes
- Implementation continuation plan (if applicable)
- Asset documentation (model walkthrough, dashboard guide)
- Open items / parked questions
- Acknowledgment receipt for client signature

### 14.4 Exit Criteria (Gate 7 → Stage 8)

- Bundle delivered and accepted
- Final invoice issued
- Internal post-mortem completed and filed
- NPS submitted

### 14.5 KPIs

- Time from last deliverable to bundle delivery (target: ≤ 5 wd)
- NPS response rate (target: ≥ 80%)
- Post-mortem completion (100% within 10 wd)

## 15. Stage 8 — Post-Engagement Care

### 15.1 Purpose

Sustain the client relationship between engagements without intrusive selling.

### 15.2 Activities

- **30-day check-in** — Personal note from EP to sponsor.
- **90-day implementation pulse** — Brief check-in on implementation status.
- **Quarterly relevant share** — Curated thought leadership, sector outlook, relevant data — addressed personally.

## 35. Diagnosis Methodology

### 35.1 The FINBERG Diagnosis Method (5 Steps)

1. **Frame** — What question are we answering? Why this question?
2. **Tree** — Decompose the question into a MECE hypothesis tree.
3. **Test** — For each hypothesis, define what evidence would confirm or refute it.
4. **Triangulate** — Collect evidence from ≥ 2 independent sources.
5. **Synthesize** — Convert findings into a clear story (the Pyramid Principle).

### 35.2 Evidence Sources

- Client interviews (≥ 8 typical)
- Client data analysis (financials, ops, customer)
- Document review (strategy, board minutes, prior reports)
- Expert calls (independent industry experts under NDA)
- Benchmarking (public sources only, with citation)
- Site visits (where relevant)

### 35.3 Triangulation Rule

No material finding is stated as fact unless supported by ≥ 2 independent sources or by primary data analysis. Single-source findings are presented as "indicated" or "reported" with the source named.

## 36. Recommendation Methodology

### 36.1 Options Discipline

Per §12.2: minimum three distinct options with full trade-off analysis.

### 36.2 The "Pre-Mortem" Discipline

For every recommended option, a structured pre-mortem is run internally:
- Imagine it is 18 months later and the option failed.
- What would have caused failure?
- What are the leading indicators?
- What pre-commitments would prevent failure?

Pre-mortem findings inform the risk section of the recommendation.

### 36.3 Decision-Quality Framework

Adapted from decision-science best practice. Six criteria for any recommendation:

1. Clear frame
2. Real alternatives
3. Reliable information
4. Clear values / trade-offs
5. Clear reasoning
6. Committed action

Each is scored 1–5 internally before recommendation goes to client.

| Format | Versioned, dated, classed |

### 61.2 The "Defensible in Court" Test

Every material claim must survive the question: "Where does this come from, and is the source reliable?"

## 63. Friday Review Process

### 63.1 Format

- 30 minutes per active engagement
- EM presents to EP + one independent partner
- Agenda: progress, quality issues, RAID, next week

### 63.2 Output

- Friday Review Note: 1 page, archived in engagement folder
- Actions logged in RAID

### 63.3 SOP-OPS-301 — Friday Review

**Inputs:** Status report, current deliverable drafts, RAID log.
**Steps:**
1. EM prepares 1-page brief 24h before review.
2. Independent partner reads pre-review.
3. 30-minute review.
4. Note filed by EOD Friday.
**Output:** Friday Review Note + actions.
**SLA:** Every active engagement, every week. Missed reviews are a P2 quality incident.

## 64. Independent Quality Reviews

### 64.3 Output

## 67. Client Communication Standards

### 67.1 Channels

- Tertiary: secure document store for artifacts
- Forbidden: personal messaging apps for confidential material

## 68. The 24-Hour Rule

Every client message is acknowledged within 24 hours (even if "I'll get back with substantive answer by X").

- Out-of-office activated for >1 day absences with delegate.
- Weekend / holiday: best-effort but standard resumes Monday.

## 69. Issue Resolution Process

### 69.1 Steps

1. Acknowledge within 24h.
2. Diagnose (often by call).
3. Propose path forward.
4. Confirm in writing.
5. Execute.
6. Follow up to confirm resolution.

### 69.2 Severity Tiers

| Tier | Description | Response Time |
|---|---|---|
| S1 | Service-affecting; client unable to proceed | 4 hours |
| S2 | Quality concern or timeline slip risk | 24 hours |
| S3 | Misalignment or minor issue | 72 hours |
| S4 | Routine question | Standard SLA |

## 70. Account Director Role

For strategic clients, an Account Director (typically a senior partner not the EP) is designated as the long-term relationship steward.

- Resolve cross-engagement issues

# PART XII — ESCALATION PROCEDURES

## 71. Escalation Triggers

A situation must be escalated when:

- A client raises dissatisfaction (any form)
- A material deliverable will miss deadline
- A material risk materializes
- A scope dispute arises
- A financial dispute arises
- A potential conflict emerges mid-engagement
- A regulatory perimeter concern emerges
- A team performance issue threatens delivery
- A safety, ethics, or compliance concern arises

## 72. Escalation Paths

|---|---|---|---|
| Client dissatisfaction | EM → EP | EP → Practice Leader | Practice Leader → CSO |
| Delivery risk | EM → EP | EP → COO | COO → CEO |
| Conflict | EM → CLO | CLO → CSO | CSO → CEO |
| Regulatory perimeter | EP → CLO | CLO → CEO | CEO → Board |
| Ethics / safety | Any → CLO | CLO → CEO | CEO → Board |

## 73. Crisis Escalation

(Cross-references Manual 01 §50.)

Crisis declared by CEO. Crisis Cell convenes within 24h. All other operations subordinate to Crisis Cell during crisis state.

## 74. Client Complaint Process

### 74.1 SOP-OPS-401 — Client Complaint

**Steps:**
1. EP acknowledges within 4 hours.
2. EP + EM document complaint in Complaint Register.
3. Practice Leader notified within 24 hours.
4. Investigation: root cause within 5 wd.
5. Proposed resolution discussed with sponsor.
6. Resolution executed.
7. 30-day follow-up.
8. Post-mortem filed.

### 74.2 Outcomes

| Outcome | Description |
|---|---|
| Resolved no fee impact | Standard delivery continues |
| Re-scope with fee neutral | Adjust scope; no fee change |
| Partial credit | Fee credit against current or future engagement |
| Full refund | Rare; CFO + CEO approval |
| Termination | EP + CEO; legal review |

# PART XV — AUTOMATION OPPORTUNITIES

## 85. Automation Strategy

### 85.1 Principles

- **Automate the repetitive, judgment-light.** Free human time for judgment-heavy work.
- **Human-in-the-loop on client-facing.** Automation never sends client-facing output without partner review (Manual 08).
- **Auditable.** Every automation has a log, an owner, and a kill switch.

### 85.2 Prioritization

Automate where (a) the activity is frequent, (b) the steps are deterministic, and (c) error cost is bounded.
